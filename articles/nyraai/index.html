Nyra AI: Qwen2 in Graal

6/22/2025

Project

Echo AI was a project that aimed to allow players in the MMO Graal Online Classic to communicate fully with OpenAI's ChatGPT within the game. Echo only ran from July 23 to July 28, 2024, before the Graal Administration Staff shut it down. Echo accumulated thousands of messages from hundreds of unique users in its short run-time. Nyra was me revisiting the project and making massive improvements to it. In this test run the project was known as Nyra. Nyra ran off and on from April 25, 2025 to May 22, 2025, before the Graal Administration Staff shut it down by banning the Nyra account, the Echo account, and my main account.


Technical

Nyra was fairly similar to her sister in how she operated, however version two was completely remade from the ground up in Python. Nyra was programmed to check for messages, read any if available, send the data to a locally hosted model of the Qwen 2 LLM, await a response, and then respond to the message in Graal Online Classic's public chat using the response it received.

Nyra had all the same features as Echo did with a few additions, such as command detection. If a player attempted to force the bot to use an in-game command, it would detect and drop the message—except for Graal Online Classic's built-in dance feature, private messaging system, and Rock-Paper-Scissors minigame.

Nyra also included a message filtering system designed to scan both incoming and outgoing messages for potentially harmful or inappropriate content. This feature aimed to automatically detect and block profanity, hate speech, or any other form of communication that violated the community guidelines or posed a threat to user safety. Despite these precautions, the filtering system was not foolproof—certain harmful messages were still able to bypass the filters due to limitations in detection accuracy or users finding ways to circumvent the system. As a result, some flagged accounts continued to engage in behavior that ultimately led to their suspension or permanent bans by the moderation team.


What Happened

The account operated steadily for quite some time, maintaining a relatively low profile aside from the occasional chat reset or being manually relocated by the administration team. Throughout this period, I remained proactive in monitoring the message logs, carefully reviewing conversations to identify any inappropriate language or terms that had managed to slip past the filters. Whenever I discovered such instances, I would immediately update the blacklist to tighten the filtering system and prevent repeat offenses.

Despite these efforts to uphold a clean and respectful environment, the situation took a turn when a player deliberately used Nyra to harass others within the game. This behavior was quickly reported by the affected players, drawing direct attention from the game's moderation team. Unfortunately, due to the severity of the incident and the nature of the reports, the account was permanently banned, marking the end of Nyra’s operation within that environment.


Final Thoughts

Nyra, and Echo before her were both amazing projects, and I have genuinely not had as much fun on any other project as I had with them. I'm interested in continuing to do more AI retrofitting, but maybe not just in the world of Graal. It was unfortunate how people used her to put others down, but I will strive to make improvements in the future.
