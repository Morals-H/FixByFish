Microscopes for Soldering

11/24/2024

Why?

Its not uncommon that I get asked about what tools I use to work on electronics, this is my recommendation for what microscope you should get based on where you are in your electronics journey. Though he majority of your soldering work as a casual technician can be done without the assistance of any magnification. With that said its nice to have when you need it. Also note that all links are Amazon affiliate links, if you purchase one of these items through my links I get a small kickback.


For Beginners

I recommend simply getting a Neiko 01902; when I was taking my IPC J-STD-001, we got to use these, and genuinely, for the price of lunch, you can't beat it. You get a 2X Zoom as well as a pair of helping hands to hold your board or wires. Neiko 01902.


For Experienced

I'd definitely say it's worth it to get a mag lamp for about 40$. It's also something I had the chance to use in my J-STD-001 course, and I was surprised with how nice it was to actually work with. You get a 10X zoom and a fairly decent light that provides 1800 lumens. ZTree Maglamp.


For Professionals

At home, I have a Kaisi TX-350E, Which is an absolutely wonderful trinocular setup with 7X to 50X magnification. With that said for the price of 360$ thats what you'd expect. Quite literally, any soldering or any inspection you will ever need to do can be done with this absolute beast of a setup. It does seem that Kaisi is no longer offering that specific model; however, now, in it's its place, you'll find the Kaisi TX-350S. From what I can tell, it's as much of a beast as its older brother, and I would likely recommend it as much as I recommend the TX-350E. Kaisi TX-350S.


Honorable Mentions

The Mechanic R75T-B1 is an fantastic scope that I used every day while I worked in a repair shop. It has a range from 7X to 45 and a wonderful ring light to illuminate anything you're working on. Honestly if I had been able to find this scope on a website I was familiar with I would have purchased it in a heartbeat instead of the Kaisi even though I have grown to love that scope.


Less-than-Honorable Mentions

the Aopick Digital Microscope. It does the job well enough that you can get away with it on most things, but many times when I've used it at my current job, I've exclaimed, "I can't see what I'm working on", because the low resolution of the camera and the lack of depth perception you get with a digital microscope. Regardless, if you want a 50$ camera microscope to maybe record a youtube video or something this'll do the job. Aopick Digital Microscope.


Final Thoughts

Most people really don't need anything better than the mag lamp, while I love having a true microscope it really isn't worth it for my home setup. Whatever you choose just make sure you get your money's worth out of it. Have fun and good luck!
